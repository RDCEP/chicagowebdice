Radiative forcing

Radiative forcing determines how much the CO2 in the atmosphere increases temperatures. It is a measure of the difference between incoming energy from the sun and the energy radiated back out to space from the earth. Any differ results in temperatures increasing. You can choose how much a given amount of CO2 in the atmosphere increases temperatures by choosing the value of the climate sensitivity.
