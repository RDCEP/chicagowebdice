Total factor productivity (TFP), $A(t),$ describes how efficiently capital and labor produce output. The initial value, $A(0),$ is set to produce 2005 output given the observed 2005 capital and labor inputs. TPF is assumed to evolve for $t>1,$ given the initial technology level according the equation:

\[ A(t)=\frac{A(t-1)}{1-A_{g}(t-1)}, \]

This model assumes that the rate of productivity growth, $A_{g}(t),$ will decrease in the coming decades. The following equation determines the growth rate of TFP:

\[ A_{g}(t)=A_{g}(0)\times exp(-\Delta_{a}\times(t-1)), \]

The default value for $\Delta_{a},$ is ___; users can choose to adjust this parameter between ___ and ____. $\Delta_{a}$ is percentage growth rate of $A_{g}(t)$, the growth rate of TFP. To see this note that

\[ \frac{d\ln A_{g}(t)}{dt}=\underset{\text{growth rate in \%}}{\underbrace{\frac{A_{g}(t+1)-A_{g}(t)}{A_{g}(t)}}=-}\Delta_{a} \]
